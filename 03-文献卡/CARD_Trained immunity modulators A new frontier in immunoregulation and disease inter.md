# Trained immunity modulators: A new frontier in immunoregulation and disease intervention

## 元数据
- **作者**: Jiao Chen et al.
- **年份**: 2025
- **期刊**: Journal of Advanced Research
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Review of molecular mechanisms underlying trained immunity, including metabolic and epigenetic reprogramming
- Classification and mechanistic analysis of trained immunity modulators (vaccines, polysaccharides, nanobiologics, endogenous mediators, etc.)
- Evaluation of dual roles of trained immunity in infectious diseases, chronic inflammatory disorders, and cancer

## 关键发现
- Trained immunity modulators include inducers (e.g., BCG, MMR, β-glucan, LPS) and suppressors, which reprogram innate immune cells via metabolic and epigenetic changes
- Trained immunity provides heterologous protection against infections but can exacerbate chronic inflammatory diseases and cancer progression
- Combining trained immunity inducers with immunotherapies (e.g., checkpoint inhibitors) shows promise for enhanced anti-tumor efficacy

## 关键洞察
> Trained immunity represents a paradigm shift in immunology, where innate immune cells acquire memory-like properties through metabolic and epigenetic reprogramming. This review comprehensively categorizes modulators (inducers and suppressors) and highlights their dual roles: beneficial in infection control and cancer therapy, but detrimental in chronic inflammation. Key modulators like BCG and β-glucan show clinical potential, especially when combined with adaptive immunotherapies, though challenges remain in balancing efficacy and safety.
