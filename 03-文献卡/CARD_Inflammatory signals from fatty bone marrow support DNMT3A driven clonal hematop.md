# Inflammatory signals from fatty bone marrow support DNMT3A driven clonal hematopoiesis

## 元数据
- **作者**: N. Zioni et al.
- **年份**: 2023
- **期刊**: Nature Communications
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Establishment of FBM models in NSG mice via sub-lethal irradiation or castration, with PPARγ inhibitor as control
- Transplantation of human DNMT3A R882H-mutated preleukemic HSPCs and normal CD34+ cells into FBM mice
- Transplantation of DNMT3A-mutant (R882H knock-in) and wild-type murine BM cells into FBM mice, including secondary transplants to assess self-renewal
- Single-cell RNA-sequencing (MARS-Seq and 10X Genomics) of LK cells from DNMT3A-mutant and wild-type mice exposed to FBM or normal BM
- Cytokine multiplex assay (FirePlex) of BM fluid and serum from FBM and control mice

## 关键发现
- FBM provides a selective advantage to human and murine DNMT3A-mutant HSPCs, increasing engraftment and self-renewal, but not to wild-type HSPCs or SRSF2-mutant cells
- DNMT3A-mutant cells exposed to FBM maintain their HSC pool and show 6-10 fold increase in HSCs compared to controls, with activation of inflammatory signaling (IFN-γ, IL-6)
- FBM secretes IL-6 (and other cytokines) in BM fluid; IL-6 levels are elevated in FBM and reduced by PPARγ inhibitor
- In vitro, IL-6 enhances self-renewal of DNMT3A-mutant HSPCs in serial replating assays; co-culture with adipocytes increases colony formation
- In vivo, neutralizing IL-6 antibodies significantly reduce the selective advantage of DNMT3A-mutant cells under FBM conditions

## 关键洞察
> Paracrine inflammatory signals from fatty bone marrow, particularly IL-6, promote the self-renewal and selective advantage of DNMT3A-mutant hematopoietic stem cells, driving clonal hematopoiesis. This effect can be inhibited by blocking the IL-6 pathway, suggesting a potential therapeutic target to mitigate age-related clonal hematopoiesis and leukemia risk.
