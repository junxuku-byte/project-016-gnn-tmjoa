# Jawbone mesenchymal stromal cells attenuate acute inflammation via hematopoietic niche reinforcement

## 元数据
- **作者**: Xinyu Wang et al.
- **年份**: 2025
- **期刊**: Frontiers in Bioengineering and Biotechnology
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Optimized enzymatic digestion to obtain single-cell suspension from mouse jawbone
- Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) to analyze cell interactions
- Tissue immunofluorescence to assess spatial distribution of MSCs and HSCs
- In vitro osteogenic and adipogenic differentiation assays
- Transwell co-culture system to study MSC effects on HSC differentiation

## 关键发现
- Jawbone MSCs and HSCs are spatially proximate and exhibit strong ligand-receptor interactions (e.g., CXCL12-CXCR4).
- Jawbone MSCs possess multilineage differentiation potential (osteogenic and adipogenic).
- Co-culture with jawbone MSCs promotes HSC differentiation into B cells, T cells, and myeloid cells, with a pronounced effect on B cells.
- Jawbone MSCs upregulate key B cell transcription factors, particularly PAX5.
- In an LPS-induced acute infection model, jawbone MSC treatment improves survival and reduces systemic inflammation (lower IFN-γ and IL-6).

## 关键洞察
> Jawbone MSCs reinforce the hematopoietic niche by secreting CXCL12 and activating PAX5, promoting HSC differentiation into B cells, thereby attenuating acute inflammation and improving survival in an LPS-induced infection model.
