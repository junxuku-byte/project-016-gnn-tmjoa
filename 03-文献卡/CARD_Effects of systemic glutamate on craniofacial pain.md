# Effects of systemic glutamate on craniofacial pain

## 元数据
- **作者**: Shimada A
- **年份**: 2015
- **期刊**: PhD dissertation, Aarhus University
- **分类**: #TMJ

## 方法
- Randomized, double-blinded, placebo-controlled trials (cross-over and parallel-group designs)
- Oral administration of MSG (150 mg/kg) or placebo (NaCl) in sugar-free lemon soda
- Intramuscular microdialysis to measure interstitial glutamate in masseter muscle
- Saliva and plasma glutamate sampling
- Pressure pain threshold (PPT) and pressure pain tolerance (PPTol) measurements using algometer

## 关键发现
- Repeated MSG intake (150 mg/kg/day for 5 days) increased interstitial glutamate in masseter muscle and plasma glutamate levels
- MSG intake was associated with increased headache and pericranial muscle tenderness in healthy individuals
- MSG did not significantly alter pressure pain thresholds or tolerance in healthy subjects or TMD patients
- Acute MSG administration did not exacerbate ongoing pain in myofascial TMD patients
- No significant correlation between interstitial, saliva, and plasma glutamate increase rates

## 关键洞察
> Systemic MSG intake elevates interstitial glutamate in jaw muscles and can induce headache and muscle tenderness in healthy individuals, but does not significantly alter mechanical pain sensitivity or exacerbate clinical pain in myofascial TMD patients. The findings suggest peripheral NMDA receptor mechanisms may mediate MSG effects, but tolerance may develop with repeated exposure.
