# Osteocyte-Intrinsic MMP13 Exacerbates Injury-Induced Temporomandibular Joint Osteoarthritis

## 元数据
- **作者**: Cristal S. Yee et al.
- **年份**: 2025
- **期刊**: Osteoarthritis and Cartilage
- **分类**: #TMJ

## 方法
- Generated DMP1-cre+/-;MMP13fl/fl mice with osteocyte-intrinsic MMP13 deficiency
- Induced TMJOA via intra-articular monosodium iodoacetate (MIA) injection in 12-week-old male mice
- Evaluated TMJ cartilage degeneration using Modified TMJ Mankin scoring on Safranin-O/Fast Green stained sections
- Assessed subchondral bone lacunocanalicular network (LCN) via Ploton silver nitrate stain
- Analyzed collagen fiber orientation using picrosirius red stain and polarized light microscopy

## 关键发现
- Osteocytic MMP13 deficiency protects TMJ from MIA-induced cartilage degeneration and subchondral bone changes
- MIA-treated control mice showed increased Modified TMJ Mankin scores and osteocyte lacunar density, while DMP1-cre+/-;MMP13fl/fl mice were protected
- Collagen organization in TMJ subchondral bone was unaffected by genotype or MIA treatment
- TRAP staining showed a significant genotype-treatment interaction effect on bone resorption
- MicroCT revealed no significant changes in TMJ subchondral bone mineralization across groups

## 关键洞察
> Osteocytic MMP13 plays a joint-specific role: it is not required for TMJ homeostasis at baseline but is necessary for injury-induced TMJOA progression. Deficiency of osteocytic MMP13 protects both TMJ and knee from injury-dependent subchondral bone changes, despite opposite effects on baseline cartilage degeneration in the two joints.
