# Efficiency of Glucosamine in Treating Temporomandibular Joint Osteoarthritis: A Meta-Analytic Umbrella Review

## 元数据
- **作者**: Sasidharan Sivakumar et al.
- **年份**: 2025
- **期刊**: Current Rheumatology Reviews
- **分类**: #TMJ

## 方法
- Umbrella review of systematic reviews
- Search of Medline (PubMed), Web of Science, Scopus, and EMBASE up to September 2023
- Screening at title, abstract, and full-text level
- Risk of bias assessment using JBI critical appraisal tool
- Meta-analysis using random effects model with Comprehensive Meta-Analysis software

## 关键发现
- Glucosamine showed non-significant but discernible effects on maximum mouth opening (SMD = 0.288, p = 0.15)
- Glucosamine showed non-significant effect on pain reduction (SMD = 0.217, p = 0.476)
- Compared to control groups (ibuprofen and tramadol), glucosamine showed slightly more favorable outcomes
- Administration for at least 3 months was associated with significant reduction in TMJ pain and increase in mouth opening
- Glucosamine and chondroitin sulfate are safe with lower risk of side effects compared to NSAIDs

## 关键洞察
> Glucosamine demonstrates a trend toward improvement in TMJ OA functional parameters (mouth opening and pain reduction) but does not reach statistical significance in pooled analysis. The efficacy is influenced by administration duration (minimum 3 months). Glucosamine is a safe adjunctive therapy with fewer side effects than NSAIDs, warranting further longitudinal studies to confirm its clinical effectiveness in TMJ OA management.
