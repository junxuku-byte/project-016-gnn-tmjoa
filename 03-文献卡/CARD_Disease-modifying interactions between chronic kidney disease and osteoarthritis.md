# Disease-modifying interactions between chronic kidney disease and osteoarthritis: a new comorbid mouse model

## 元数据
- **作者**: Julovi SM et al.
- **年份**: 2023
- **期刊**: RMD Open
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Male C57BL/6 mice underwent 5/6 nephrectomy (5/6Nx) to induce CKD, with or without destabilisation of medial meniscus (DMM) to induce OA, or sham surgeries.
- OA pathology and CKD were assessed 12 weeks post-induction using blinded histology scoring, micro-CT, immunohistochemistry for osteoclast and MMP-13 activity, and serum analysis of bone metabolic markers.
- Renal function was evaluated via urine protein, creatinine, blood urea, and histology; subchondral bone morphology was analyzed via micro-CT; joint histopathology was scored for cartilage damage, osteophytes, synovitis, and subchondral bone changes.

## 关键发现
- 5/6Nx alone did not induce cartilage pathology but increased synovial MMP-13 expression and subchondral bone osteoclastic activity, reducing subchondral trabecular bone volume and mineral density.
- DMM alone induced typical OA pathology (cartilage damage, subchondral bone sclerosis, osteophytes, synovitis) and also caused renal fibrosis and proteinuria.
- 5/6Nx significantly reduced DMM-induced cartilage pathology and osteophyte formation, associated with reduced subchondral bone volume and density and inhibition of osteoclast activation.
- DMM in 5/6Nx mice reduced serum phosphate and calcium levels, indicating altered bone metabolism.
- Bidirectional disease-modifying interactions were observed: CKD altered OA progression, and OA affected renal function and morphology.

## 关键洞察
> This study provides the first preclinical comorbid CKD-OA model, demonstrating that CKD-induced subchondral bone resorption and reduced bone mineral density can protect against OA-related cartilage damage and osteophyte formation, while OA itself can induce renal fibrosis and dysfunction, highlighting complex bidirectional cross-talk between the two diseases.
