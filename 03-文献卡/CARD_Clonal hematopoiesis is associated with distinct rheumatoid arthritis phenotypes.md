# Clonal hematopoiesis is associated with distinct rheumatoid arthritis phenotypes

## 元数据
- **作者**: Emil Hiitola et al.
- **年份**: 2025
- **期刊**: Science Advances
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Whole-exome sequencing (WES) and SNP array analysis in FINRISK cohort (n=10,089) to detect CHIP, lymphoid driver variants, and mosaic chromosomal alterations (mCAs).
- SNP array-based CHIP hotspot annotation in FinnGen cohort (n=520,210) with manual curation of intensity plots.
- Targeted next-generation sequencing (NGS) of 65 myeloid genes in 573 newly diagnosed, untreated RA patients and 163 healthy controls.
- Multivariable logistic regression and Cox proportional hazards models adjusted for age, sex, smoking, and genetic ancestry.
- Comparison of CHIP prevalence and clinical parameters (CRP, ESR, DAS28, HAQ, seropositivity) across RA subtypes.

## 关键发现
- CHIP was associated with prevalent RA in FINRISK (OR 2.06, P=0.029) and FinnGen (OR 1.49, P<0.001).
- In FinnGen, CHIP was associated with inferior overall survival in participants with RA (HR 1.92, P=0.011).
- In newly diagnosed RA, DNMT3A-mutated seropositive patients had higher inflammatory markers and disease activity.
- TET2 mutations were enriched in seronegative RA (P=0.009).
- Non-DNMT3A CHIP was associated with seronegative RA in FinnGen (OR 3.60, P<0.001).

## 关键洞察
> CHIP, particularly DNMT3A and TET2 mutations, shows context-dependent associations with RA subtypes: DNMT3A linked to more severe seropositive RA, while TET2 enriched in seronegative RA, suggesting potential for personalized anti-inflammatory therapy (e.g., IL-1β inhibition) based on CHIP status.
