# Clonal Hematopoiesis and Risk of Atherosclerotic Cardiovascular Disease

## 元数据
- **作者**: S. Jaiswal et al.
- **年份**: 2017
- **期刊**: The New England Journal of Medicine
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Whole-exome sequencing to detect CHIP in peripheral-blood cells from four case–control studies (4726 coronary heart disease cases, 3529 controls)
- Nested case–control analysis from two prospective cohorts (BioImage and Malmö Diet and Cancer) and two retrospective case–control studies (ATVB and PROMIS) for early-onset myocardial infarction
- Gene-level analysis for DNMT3A, TET2, ASXL1, and JAK2 mutations
- Assessment of coronary-artery calcification via cardiac CT in BioImage participants
- Murine model: bone marrow transplantation from Tet2 knockout (homozygous or heterozygous) mice into atherosclerosis-prone Ldlr knockout mice, followed by high-cholesterol diet and lesion quantification

## 关键发现
- CHIP carriers had 1.9 times the risk of coronary heart disease (95% CI, 1.4 to 2.7) in prospective cohorts and 4.0 times the risk of early-onset myocardial infarction (95% CI, 2.4 to 6.7) in retrospective studies
- Mutations in DNMT3A, TET2, ASXL1, and JAK2 were individually associated with coronary heart disease; JAK2 V617F conferred 12.1 times the risk
- CHIP carriers had higher coronary-artery calcification scores; those with variant allele fraction ≥10% had 12 times the risk of high calcification
- Mice engrafted with Tet2 knockout bone marrow developed larger atherosclerotic lesions in aortic root and descending aorta compared with controls
- Tet2 knockout macrophages showed elevated expression of CXC chemokines (e.g., Cxcl1, Cxcl2, Cxcl3) and proinflammatory cytokines (Il1b, Il6); humans with TET2 mutations had higher plasma interleukin-8 levels

## 关键洞察
> CHIP, defined by somatic mutations in hematopoietic cells (e.g., DNMT3A, TET2, ASXL1, JAK2), is associated with increased risk of coronary heart disease and early-onset myocardial infarction, likely through a causal mechanism involving enhanced macrophage inflammation and chemokine expression, as demonstrated by Tet2 loss in murine models.
