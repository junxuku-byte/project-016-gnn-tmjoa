# Inhibition of FGFR Signaling Partially Rescues Osteoarthritis in Mice Overexpressing High Molecular Weight FGF2 Isoforms

## 元数据
- **作者**: Liping Xiao et al.
- **年份**: 2020
- **期刊**: Endocrinology
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Transgenic mouse model (HMWTg) overexpressing HMWFGF2 isoforms in osteoblast lineage
- Daily subcutaneous injection of FGFR inhibitor NVP-BGJ398 (2 mg/kg) or vehicle for 13 weeks starting at 21 days of age
- Micro-computed tomography (microCT) for subchondral bone morphometry
- Histological assessment with Safranin-O staining and OARSI scoring
- Alkaline phosphatase staining for calcified cartilage

## 关键发现
- BGJ treatment partially rescued irregular shape and thinning of subchondral bone in HMWTg mice, with increased BV/TV and Tb.Th in males
- BGJ restored articular cartilage thickness and proteoglycan content in HMWTg mice
- BGJ reduced OA degradative enzymes (MMP13, ADAMTS5) and hypertrophic markers (Col10, SOX9) in HMWTg joints
- BGJ decreased elevated pFGFR1 and FGF23 expression in articular cartilage of HMWTg mice
- BGJ rescued aberrant Wnt signaling by reducing WNT7B, pLRP5, AXIN2, pGSK3β, active β-catenin, and increasing DKK1 and SOST in HMWTg cartilage

## 关键洞察
> Overexpression of HMWFGF2 isoforms in preosteoblasts leads to osteoarthropathy in mice, which can be partially rescued by FGFR inhibitor NVP-BGJ398 through reduction of activated Wnt signaling, suggesting FGFR blockade as a potential therapeutic strategy for OA in XLH and related conditions.
