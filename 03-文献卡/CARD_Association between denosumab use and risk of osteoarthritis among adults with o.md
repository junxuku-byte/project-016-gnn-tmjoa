# Association between denosumab use and risk of osteoarthritis among adults with osteoporosis in a real-world cohort

## 元数据
- **作者**: Zhaohua Zhu et al.
- **年份**: 2026
- **期刊**: Osteoarthritis and Cartilage
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- New-user, active-comparator retrospective real-world cohort study using electronic health records from the TriNetX Network.
- Propensity-score matching (PSM) to balance potential confounders between denosumab and bisphosphonate users.
- Cox proportional hazards models to estimate hazard ratios (HRs) and 95% confidence intervals (CIs) for incident knee and hip OA.

## 关键发现
- Denosumab use was associated with a reduced risk of any OA (HR 0.96, 95% CI 0.94-0.98) and knee OA (HR 0.87, 95% CI 0.84-0.91) compared with bisphosphonates.
- No significant association was found for hip OA (HR 0.98, 95% CI 0.94-1.03) or thumb carpometacarpal OA (HR 0.94, 95% CI 0.87-1.01).
- The association was more pronounced among older adults (≥65 years), females, and individuals of Asian ethnicity.

## 关键洞察
> Denosumab, a RANKL inhibitor used for osteoporosis, may reduce the risk of incident knee OA compared to bisphosphonates, particularly in older, female, and Asian populations, suggesting a potential disease-modifying effect for knee OA that warrants further clinical trials.
