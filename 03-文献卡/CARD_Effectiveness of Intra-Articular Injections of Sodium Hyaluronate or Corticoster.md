# Effectiveness of Intra-Articular Injections of Sodium Hyaluronate or Corticosteroids for Intracapsular Temporomandibular Disorders: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

## 元数据
- **作者**: Marlon A. Moldez et al.
- **年份**: 2018
- **期刊**: Journal of Oral & Facial Pain and Headache
- **分类**: #TMJ

## 方法
- Systematic review and meta-analysis of single- or double-blinded randomized controlled trials (RCTs) comparing NaH or CS injections to each other or placebo for intracapsular TMD due to osteoarthritis and/or internal joint derangement.
- Electronic searches of MEDLINE (PubMed), Web of Science, and Cochrane Library conducted on March 17, 2015, with an update on June 7, 2017.
- Three reviewers independently extracted data and assessed risk of bias using Cochrane Handbook methods.
- Meta-analyses performed for change in VAS pain (standardized difference in means) and number of patients with improved symptoms (risk ratios), using fixed- or random-effects models based on heterogeneity.

## 关键发现
- Pooled results showed no significant difference in short-term (4-6 weeks) or long-term (6 months to 2 years) pain improvement with NaH compared to CS.
- NaH was significantly better than placebo in number of responders (improvement of symptoms) in one study (Hepguler et al, 2002).
- No significant difference in number of responders between NaH and CS.
- Most studies had unclear risk of bias; one study had high risk of bias due to large dropouts.
- Side effects were mild and self-limiting, with some post-injection pain reported for NaH.

## 关键洞察
> This systematic review and meta-analysis found no significant difference in pain reduction between intra-articular injections of sodium hyaluronate and corticosteroids for intracapsular TMD, though NaH showed some benefit over placebo. The evidence is limited by small sample sizes, unclear risk of bias in most studies, and heterogeneity in injection protocols and outcome measures. Further research is needed to determine minimum effective doses and long-term side effects.
