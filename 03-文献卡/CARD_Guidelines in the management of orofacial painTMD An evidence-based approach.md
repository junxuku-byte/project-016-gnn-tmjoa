# Guidelines in the management of orofacial pain/TMD: An evidence-based approach

## 元数据
- **作者**: Anders Wänman et al.
- **年份**: 2015
- **期刊**: Nor Tannlegeforen Tid
- **分类**: #TMJ

## 方法
- Systematic literature search in Medline/PubMed, Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, DARE, HTA Database, and Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials (1965–2014).
- Pair-wise audit of publications by 12 specialists using GRADE methodology.
- Health economic analysis of cost-effectiveness.
- Expert panel ranking of condition severity and treatment priority (1–10 scale).

## 关键发现
- Counseling and behavioral approaches are first-line interventions for TMD.
- Stabilization appliances and jaw exercises have evidence of effect and cost-effectiveness, and may be combined with NSAIDs for limited periods.
- Occlusal adjustment is contraindicated in acute TMJ arthralgia, acute arthritis, and dysocclusion (phantom bite).
- Low-level laser and TENS have low or no evidence for TMD treatment.
- Surgical interventions (arthroscopy, discectomy, arthrocentesis) are reserved for patients refractory to conservative treatment for at least 6 months.

## 关键洞察
> The Swedish national guidelines provide a comprehensive, evidence-based priority ranking for TMD treatments, integrating clinical effectiveness, health economics, and condition severity. Key recommendations emphasize reversible, conservative treatments (behavioral therapy, stabilization splints, jaw exercises) as first-line, while irreversible procedures (occlusal adjustment, surgery) are reserved for specific, refractory cases. The guidelines highlight the importance of biopsychosocial assessment due to common comorbidities with widespread pain and depression.
