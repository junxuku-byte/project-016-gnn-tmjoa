# Single-Cell Transcriptomics Reveals Riluzole as an Osteoarthritis Candidate Drug via OB-NE Signaling Modulation and CTSS/NOS1 Inhibition

## 元数据
- **作者**: Kun Liu et al.
- **年份**: 2026
- **期刊**: Drug Design, Development and Therapy
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Single-cell RNA sequencing of human femoral head tissue from OA patients and controls
- Integrated bioinformatics analysis including differential expression, GO enrichment, and cell communication analysis (CellChat)
- Network proximity-based drug repositioning integrating scRNA-seq data with drug-target and protein-protein interaction networks
- Mendelian randomization analysis to assess causal relationships between drug targets and OA risk
- In vivo validation using a triclocarban (TCC)-induced zebrafish OA model treated with riluzole

## 关键发现
- Osteoblasts (OBs) were identified as key OA-associated cells, with differentially expressed genes enriched in neutrophil (NE)-mediated immune pathways.
- Enhanced bidirectional OB-NE signaling in OA was observed, primarily mediated by CXCL12-CXCR4 and RETN-CAP1 ligand-receptor pairs.
- Riluzole was identified as a top drug candidate via network-based drug repositioning.
- Mendelian randomization showed an inverse causal association between riluzole's target SLC7A11 expression and OA risk (OR=0.84, 95% CI=0.72–0.98, P=0.027).
- In a zebrafish OA model, riluzole treatment significantly reduced joint degeneration and downregulated CTSS, NOS1, and key components of the MAPK/ERK pathway.

## 关键洞察
> This study provides a comprehensive framework for precision drug repurposing in OA by integrating single-cell transcriptomics, cell communication analysis, and network-based drug screening. It highlights riluzole, an FDA-approved ALS drug, as a promising candidate for OA treatment, potentially acting through modulation of OB-NE signaling and inhibition of CTSS and NOS1, leading to suppression of the MAPK/ERK pathway.
