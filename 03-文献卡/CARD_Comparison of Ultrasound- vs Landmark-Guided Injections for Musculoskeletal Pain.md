# Comparison of Ultrasound- vs Landmark-Guided Injections for Musculoskeletal Pain: An Umbrella Review

## 元数据
- **作者**: Peng-Chieh Shen et al.
- **年份**: 2024
- **期刊**: Journal of Rehabilitation Medicine
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Umbrella review of systematic reviews and meta-analyses
- Search of PubMed, EMBASE, MEDLINE, and Web of Science from inception to March 2024
- Quality assessment using AMSTAR2 instrument
- Data extraction and synthesis following PRISMA guidelines

## 关键发现
- Ultrasound-guided injections generally more accurate than landmark-guided injections, especially in shoulder joint, bicipital groove, wrist, hip, and knee
- Pain relief and functional outcomes varied across sites; ultrasound guidance notably effective for bicipital groove, wrist, hip, and knee injections
- Both techniques showed low incidence of adverse effects
- High heterogeneity and variable quality among included reviews (3 high, 1 moderate, 7 low, 6 critically low quality)

## 关键洞察
> Ultrasound guidance improves injection accuracy across multiple musculoskeletal sites, but its clinical benefit for pain and function is inconsistent and site-dependent, with strongest evidence for bicipital groove, wrist, hip, and knee injections.
