# INTRA-ARTICULAR PHARMACOLOGICAL INJECTIONS FOR TEMPOROMANDIBULAR JOINT OSTEOARTHRITIS ARE COMPARABLE TO PLACEBO

## 元数据
- **作者**: Elliot Abt
- **年份**: 2024
- **期刊**: Journal of Evidence-Based Dental Practice
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Systematic review with network meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials
- Search of PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials, and gray literature via ClinicalTrials.gov
- Two reviewers independently assessed risk of bias using dimensions of quality in RCTs
- Direct and indirect comparisons via network meta-analysis

## 关键发现
- Intra-articular injections of CCS, HA, or PRP had no effect on improving pain reduction compared with placebo
- CCS injection was significantly worse than placebo for short-term pain reduction (MD = 1.73; 95% CI 0.37-3.09)
- Most results were not statistically significant; PRP vs placebo MD = -0.23 (95% CI -2.49 to 2.04)
- Studies were generally small and underpowered, with considerable heterogeneity

## 关键洞察
> Current best evidence fails to show a benefit of intra-articular injections (CCS, HA, PRP) over placebo for TMJ OA pain reduction; clinicians should use caution with these injections. Larger, high-quality RCTs are needed to reduce uncertainty.
