# Transforming TMJ pain relief: Hyaluronic acid's efficacy in focus – a comprehensive systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials

## 元数据
- **作者**: Flores-Fraile J et al.
- **年份**: 2025
- **期刊**: Dental and Medical Problems
- **分类**: #TMJ

## 方法
- Systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials (RCTs)
- Search in Web of Science, Cochrane Library, MEDLINE, PubMed (May 2014–May 2023)
- PICOS framework: Population (adult TMJ pain/dysfunction), Intervention (HA injection), Comparison (other materials or no intervention), Outcome (pain relief, MMO, function), Study design (RCTs)
- Risk of bias assessed using Cochrane method; evidence quality graded with GRADE
- Meta-analysis using fixed-effects model, RR with 95% CI, heterogeneity via I² and χ²

## 关键发现
- HA is safe and effective for reducing TMJ pain and improving maximum mouth opening (MMO)
- Overall meta-analysis showed no statistical difference between HA and other treatments in improving stomatognathic function (RR = 1.01, 95% CI: 0.92–1.11, p = 0.81, I² = 0%)
- HA demonstrated superior long-term results compared to platelet-rich plasma (PRP)
- Combination of arthrocentesis with HA injection yielded better chewing efficiency and quality of life than HA alone
- HA was more effective than betamethasone or tenoxicam for pain relief, but at higher cost

## 关键洞察
> Hyaluronic acid is a safe and effective intra-articular treatment for TMJ disorders, particularly for pain reduction and improving mouth opening. While comparable to other injectables in short-term efficacy, HA shows superior long-term outcomes versus PRP. The meta-analysis found no significant heterogeneity across studies, supporting consistency in findings. However, HA's higher cost and the potential superiority of PRP in some contexts should be considered in clinical decision-making.
