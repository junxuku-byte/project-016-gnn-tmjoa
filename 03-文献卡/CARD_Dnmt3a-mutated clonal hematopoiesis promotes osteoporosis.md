# Dnmt3a-mutated clonal hematopoiesis promotes osteoporosis

## 元数据
- **作者**: Peter Geon Kim et al.
- **年份**: 2021
- **期刊**: Journal of Experimental Medicine
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Analysis of UK Biobank exome sequencing data for association between CHIP and osteoporosis diagnoses and bone mineral density
- Murine bone marrow transplantation models with hematopoietic-specific Dnmt3a or Tet2 knockout
- Micro-computed tomography (µCT) for bone mass assessment
- Histological analysis for osteoclast quantification
- Ex vivo osteoclast differentiation assays

## 关键发现
- CHIP is associated with increased incident osteoporosis diagnoses (HR 1.44, 95% CI 1.22-1.72) and decreased estimated bone mineral density in humans
- Hematopoietic-specific Dnmt3a mutations in mice decrease trabecular and cortical bone mass via increased osteoclastogenesis
- Dnmt3a-mutant macrophages secrete proinflammatory cytokines, including IL-20, in a non-cell-autonomous manner to promote osteoclast differentiation
- IL-20 neutralization or alendronate treatment reverses bone loss in Dnmt3a-mutant mice
- Dnmt3a loss leads to hypomethylation, increased chromatin accessibility, and enhanced Irf3-NF-κB binding at the Il20 locus

## 关键洞察
> CHIP mutations, particularly in DNMT3A, causally promote osteoporosis through a non-cell-autonomous mechanism where mutant macrophages secrete IL-20 via Irf3-NF-κB signaling, driving osteoclastogenesis and bone loss, which can be therapeutically targeted by IL-20 neutralization or bisphosphonates.
