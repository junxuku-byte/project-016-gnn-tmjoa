# Clinical effectiveness of manual therapy for the management of musculoskeletal and non-musculoskeletal conditions: systematic review and update of UK evidence report

## 元数据
- **作者**: Clar C et al.
- **年份**: 2014
- **期刊**: Chiropractic & Manual Therapies
- **分类**: #general

## 方法
- Systematic review with literature search of over 10 databases (August 2011, updated March 2013).
- Inclusion of systematic reviews, RCTs, controlled clinical trials, cohort studies, and qualitative studies.
- Quality assessment using AMSTAR, Cochrane Risk of Bias, CRD checklist, and CASP.
- Evidence rating based on consistency, study size, and quality (high, moderate, inconclusive).
- Comparison of new evidence against ratings from the Bronfort et al. UK evidence report.

## 关键发现
- Most inconclusive or moderate evidence ratings from the UK evidence report were confirmed.
- Evidence ratings changed from inconclusive to moderate in only three cases: manipulation/mobilisation with exercise for rotator cuff disorder, spinal mobilisation for cervicogenic headache, and mobilisation for miscellaneous headache.
- Evidence for non-musculoskeletal conditions was mostly rated as inconclusive.
- Overall, limited high-quality evidence for manual therapy effectiveness; most evidence was low to moderate quality and inconsistent.

## 关键洞察
> The review confirms that manual therapy evidence remains largely inconclusive for many conditions, with only a few upgrades from inconclusive to moderate. High-quality evidence is scarce, and methodological diversity limits conclusions. Further research is needed, particularly for non-musculoskeletal conditions.
