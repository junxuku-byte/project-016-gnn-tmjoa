# Can treatment with chondroitin and glucosamine sulphate prevent changes in the articular disc caused by temporomandibular joint osteoarthritis?

## 元数据
- **作者**: Giulia Giacomini Malaguez et al.
- **年份**: 2024
- **期刊**: Journal of Oral Rehabilitation
- **分类**: #TMJ

## 方法
- 36 male New Zealand rabbits divided into control, osteoarthritis, and treatment groups
- Osteoarthritis induced by intra-articular injection of sodium monoiodoacetate
- Treatment group received subcutaneous chondroitin and glucosamine sulphate (7.5 mg/kg) every 3 days starting 10 days post-induction
- Collagen quantification via biochemical analysis (Sircol assay) and histological analysis (PicroSirius Red staining under polarized light)
- Serum TNF-α levels measured by enzyme immunoassay (ELISA)

## 关键发现
- Treatment group showed significantly increased total collagen area in articular disc compared to control and osteoarthritis groups
- No significant difference in collagen concentration between groups, but significant reduction over time (40 vs 100 days)
- Serum TNF-α levels significantly lower in treatment group compared to osteoarthritis group post-treatment
- Histological analysis showed better preservation of collagen fiber organization in treatment group

## 关键洞察
> CGS treatment delayed collagen degeneration in TMJ articular disc and reduced serum TNF-α levels, indicating a preventive and anti-inflammatory effect on OA progression, potentially by maintaining disc structure and modulating inflammation.
